Location and Times

G/013 at 20:15 every Thursday night.

Course Content

Each lecture shall be a standalone event, and you should feel free to come along to any even if you have not been to the preceeding lectures.

Week 4: Version Control and Teamwork

A short session on what version control is and where you would want to use it, with more explanation of how to use Git, a popular VCS. Additionally, how to use version control for team projects without making your colleagues hate your guts and try to kill you as you sleep.

Notes: TeX, pdf.

Week 7: Public Key Cryptography and Key Signing Party

What public-key crypto is and where it's useful and how the concept of a web-of-trust enables greater security in decentralised systems. After this, there shall be an opportunity for people with GPG/PGP keys to have them signed by producing proof of identity and their key fingerprint.

Week 8: Software Testing

Why testing is good and when you should do it. Will cover different testing strategies and in which situations you should use different approaches. Focus will be on unit testing and testing Java projects with Spring.

Week 9: Writing Readable Code

How to write code that you will be able to understand when you come back to it after a long time, and how to adjust your coding style when working with others.
